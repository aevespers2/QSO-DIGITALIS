## Material obstructions

1. **Identity collapse:** source, temporal, interpretation, projection, envelope, transport, receipt, disposition, and consumption identities can be conflated.
2. **Temporal ownership overlap:** Digitalis could silently recalculate freshness or replay status owned elsewhere.
3. **Generic envelope ownership conflict:** multiple repositories describe generic envelopes, registries, canonicalization, and migrations.
4. **Exchange/transport overlap:** the field architecture can be read as overlapping Bridge transport authority.
5. **Evidence-store/canonical-state overlap:** content-addressed derived artifacts can be mistaken for Repository `1` canonical operational state.
6. **Projection/capability confusion:** a purpose-limited view can be mistaken for permission to act.
7. **Privacy downgrade:** redaction, aggregation, or derivation can weaken source restrictions or enable sensitive inference.
8. **Correction/cache divergence:** revoked or corrected evidence can remain active downstream.
9. **Terminology ambiguity:** “Digital Consciousness Field” can imply unsupported consciousness, personhood, or authority claims.

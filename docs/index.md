# QSO-DIGITALIS

QSO-DIGITALIS is a **charter-stage contract candidate** for bounded interpretation, policy projection, and content-addressed evidence exchange within the A.L.I.S.T.A.I.R.E. Quantum State Object portfolio. It is being evaluated as the layer that preserves immutable source identity while attaching separately identified temporal/domain assessments and producing purpose-limited, non-executable consumer views.

!!! warning "Current status"
    The repository does not contain an approved field contract, production runtime, transport service, persistent multi-user store, signing service, deployment target, or autonomous authority. Draft PR #2 is an architecture/scaffold candidate under review. Its roadmap and materializer are proposal artifacts, not proof of implementation.

## Why this repository exists

The portfolio needs a stable boundary between systems that retrieve evidence, systems that interpret or limit evidence for a specific purpose, systems that transport it, systems that decide canonical admission, and systems that execute local cognition or coordinate experiments.

The lowest-coupling candidate now under review is:

- QSO-SEEKER prepares accepted inert source-observation records.
- A separately governed temporal authority assesses clock identity, freshness, ordering, uncertainty, and replay risk.
- QSO-DIGITALIS references those immutable inputs, declares domain interpretations, and creates purpose-, sensitivity-, retention-, and consumer-limited projections and exchange envelopes.
- Bridge admits and transports approved artifacts and produces delivery receipts.
- Repository `1` performs independent quarantine, capability, canonical-state, revocation, correction, checkpoint, and recovery decisions.
- QuantumStateObjects and QSO-FABRIC consume exact accepted versions and hashes under separate capabilities.
- QSO-STUDIO and AionUi display source, interpretation, projection, transport, and disposition states without becoming authority.

This role is proposed, not approved. QSO-DIGITALIS must not retrieve sources, silently redefine temporal truth, issue operational capabilities, own generic network transport, become Repository `1` canonical state, execute runtimes, approve experiments, or infer literal consciousness from its architectural name.

## Material obstruction

The portfolio currently risks collapsing source record identity, temporal assessment, domain interpretation, policy projection, transport artifact, delivery receipt, Repository `1` disposition, and consumer display into one misleading “evidence” identity. That can make transport success look like truth, a derived interpretation look like source evidence, or a projection look like an operational capability.

The candidate repair keeps every stage separately identified and hash-bound, with explicit typed references, transformation declarations, privacy/retention inheritance, correction and revocation propagation, and pairwise/triple-overlap fixtures.

## Design principles

1. **Schema before runtime.** Contracts, fixtures, negative cases, and migrations must precede implementation.
2. **Source preservation.** Interpretations and projections reference immutable source records; they never overwrite them.
3. **Evidence, not execution.** Field records must remain inert and must never grant code, credential, repository, payment, or deployment authority.
4. **Identity separation.** Source, temporal, interpretation, projection, envelope, transport, receipt, disposition, and consumption identities remain distinct.
5. **Capability separation.** Publish, interpret, project, transport, discover, subscribe, read, acknowledge, revoke, and administer are distinct permissions.
6. **Purpose limitation.** Every view is constrained by approved purpose, sensitivity, retention, source class, consent, and consumer scope.
7. **Content-addressed provenance.** Consumers bind to explicit versions, canonicalization methods, domain-separated hashes, and lineage rather than mutable names alone.
8. **Declared transformations.** Normalization, redaction, aggregation, derivation, and repackaging are explicit and reproducible.
9. **Fail closed.** Unknown schema versions, invalid hashes, stale assessments, undeclared transformations, expired records, revoked access, or missing policy evidence produce rejection or `UNKNOWN`.
10. **Human final review.** Consequential promotion, action, merge, release, and deployment remain separately authorized.
